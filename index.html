How Does the Estrus Cycle Affect Female Mice’s Activity and Body Temperature?

Solid Line: Activity Level | Dashed Line: Temperature | Shaded Area: Estrus Period

Design Rationale

Our visualization is designed to answer the question: "How does the estrus cycle influence female mice’s activity and body temperature, and how does that differ from male mice?"

We chose a dual-line time series plot to represent the two physiological variables—activity level and core body temperature—over time, with separate y-axes for clarity. Activity is represented by a solid steelblue line, and temperature by a dashed tomato line, helping users visually distinguish the two metrics.

To support pattern recognition, I added shaded pink rectangles to highlight estrus periods (every 4 days starting from day 2), guiding viewers to key windows of biological change. A dropdown menu allows users to isolate individual mice and view patterns more clearly, avoiding overplotting. We included tooltip hovers to provide exact numerical values for each minute, and a brushing tool to enable zooming into specific time segments.

I considered alternatives like area charts and stacked bar plots, but found that line charts offered better continuity and rhythm detection for time-series data. Additionally, we debated overlaying all mice at once but decided to start with one-at-a-time views to reduce visual clutter and support interpretability.

We estimate that we spent roughly 5 people-hours on this project. The most time-consuming parts were:

  • Implementing dual-axis interactivity and brushing in D3.js.
  • Fine-tuning the design and user experience, including axis labeling, hover tooltips, and estrus day overlays.

Through multiple iterations and peer testing, we refined our layout, interactions, and labeling to create an expressive and engaging visualization that clearly communicates our biological question.
